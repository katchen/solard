Here are some fast facts on the product: What is it? Organic Waste: Food scraps, seed husks, and other plants Mycelium: Mushrooms do not reproduce by seed, but by spores. When the spores reproduce, they produce single-celled, interwoven structures called hyphae (also known as Shiro). Together, the masses of hyphae are called Mycelium.   How is it made?
  1. The organic waste (agricultural waste) is cleaned using a steam heat process for sterilization. (Currently, McIntyre and Bayer are looking into replacing the steam-heat process with a treatment made from cinnamon-bark oil, thyme oil, oregano oil and lemongrass oil to decrease the energy required to sterilize the waste).
  2. This sterilized waste is then put in a container with growing Mycelium for about 5-10 days, until the desired Mycelium density is acquired. Since Mycelium is a mushroom and does not require photosynthesis, it grows in the dark at room temperature and without any water or petrochemicals.  As it grows, Mycelium acts as glue weaving between the organic waste, resulting in the desired structure. No energy is required for shaping the product because it is shaped as it grows into its plastic mold, which can be customized for preference.
  3. The final product is heat-treated to stop the spores from continuing from growing.
Why choose EcoCradle over Styrofoam?
  • EcoCradle is made using only one-eighth of the amount of energy Styrofoam uses in its manufacturing process and one-tenth the amount of carbon dioxide is released!
  • EcoCradle is:
    • fire resistant
    • moisture resistant
    • vapor resistant
    • hypoallergenic
  • EcoCradle decomposes within one month after it is buried in the ground.
